## Trivialities of "stanardized code"

As an inexperienced 'code writer' (otherwise known as a maturing software engineer), it is very typical to get into personal habits when writing programs. Some may declare every variable right before they're used, while others organize every variable declaration at the top (the latter being myself). One person may view For-loops as their arch nemesis, while another has them littered everywhere throughout the page. There are countless examples of varying differences between how each person chooses to write their code, and we each have our reasons to do so. Such phenomena generally don't exhibit negative consequences when small programs are developed on an individual scale, like in an isolated bubble, but you can start to see an emerging issue when larger and more sophisticated projects include dozens or more individuals. Each developer brings their own unique stylistic choices to the table, and while technically any code that 'gets the job done' will probably suffice, anyone who has ever tried to debug a friend's project could testify that following someone else's syntax is almost equivalent to learning a new language on-the-fly. So, whats the easiest way out of this hole we've dug?
