# Hiking Trail Guide

### Overview
*The* *problem*: Being in Hawaii, finding new and exciting trails can be a challenge. Understanding the difficulty of these trails, the length, ensuring you’re prepared for the trail and navigating the trail takes time. Not to mention the potential dangers of weather conditions that affect these trails. 

*The* *solution*: Develop a comprehensive Hiking Trail Guide App that serves as a one-stop solution for Hawaii hikers looking to find a trail that matches their desired skills and preferences. The app will feature a database of hiking trails available in Hawaii (mostly in Oahu) that will provide detailed information on the hiking location

### Approach
*Trail* *finder*: Enables filtering of trails by difficulty, length, elevation, and more, matching users to ideal paths. Even finding out where to park or best times to go. Some of these trails also have “off-limits” to inform hikers.

*Real-time* *updates*: Offers updates on trail conditions, weather forecasts, and alerts about potential hazards.

*Community* *feedback*: Offer a community feedback section on the hiking trail

# Kahoot-style Review Game

### Overview
*The* *problem*: Encouraging students to actively participate in a classroom review or concept-check for an upcoming quiz or exam can sometimes pose a challenge. 

*The* *solution*: Kahoot is a fun, interactive, and engaging way for an entire classroom or large group of people to review key concepts and terminology for any course. A public leaderboard for any given quizlet offers incentives for students to answer questions as quickly and accurately as possible to gain points and see themselves rise to the top of the board. Bright and eye-catching colors, catchy countdown music, and a competitive atmosphere will ensure any student will prepare for and look forward to any Kahoot review game played in future class sessions for all the bragging rights!

### Approach
Players in a Kahoot game each individually log on to a site with their unique username or ‘nickname’ for that session. 

*Game*: Each game will have a multiple-choice quiz with an amount of questions typically ranging from 10-20, depending on how many questions the host enters. When all of the players join and the quiz is started, each question will be shown for 5 seconds before the options for answers appear. This allows each player to enough time to comprehend the question before given a chance to answer, since this is a time-based game. 

After each answering period is over, an updated scoreboard is shown to the class with the top scores on the main screen, and a relative player standing on each individual player’s devices.

*Scoring*: There is a limited amount of time for each question to be answered. The quicker a player answers correctly, the more points they will get for a correct answer. No points will be awarded for an incorrect answer or no answer. 

There will be a scoring multiplier for consecutively answering correctly. This will allow players who have “fallen behind” to potentially catch back up by ensuring they correctly answer any upcoming questions. This also helps to emphasize the importance of answering correctly, not just quickly.
