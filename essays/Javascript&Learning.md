## How did my first encounter with Javascript go?

A few weeks have already passed since my introduction into Javascript. I have experienced writing with Java and a little bit of Python in the past so there are certain attributes that are familiar. There has been a slight learning curve with Javascript, but nothing steep enough that cannot be overcome. The main sticking points that have me stuck are syntax related issues along with the inability to understand the inner workings of a new programming language well enough to manipulate variables and data structures intuitively. 

## Is there such thing as a superior language?

As far as my thoughts on if Javascript is generally a more superior programming language in comparison to others, I cannot say. There is still much to learn about all programming languages and how they compare or differ from one another. I can relate it to learning a new spoken language from another culture. It is difficult to assess if any one language is inherently superior to another, especially when the person making the assessment is still a novice.

##Learning how to learn

When mentioning the strategy being implemented for me to learn Javascript, I can say it probably has more benefits than drawbacks in terms of how well it teaches material; this strategy being “athletic software engineering”. The key principle being implemented here is learning by doing. I’m certain everybody has heard the saying “practice makes perfect”, but there is likely a reason why that is a popular idiom to use. Understanding a concept on paper and applying the concept in practice are two different ball games, and the process of learning requires that the student takes part in both for the best results. For this reason, applying what I have recently learned on paper inside of a “Workout of the Day” practice format allows me to reinforce my understanding of certain concepts while uncovering hidden pits of confusion that need further exploration. Although it may be difficult to keep up with in the short term, I’m sure the long term benefits of this challenge will pay dividends for many years to come.
